Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the development of computer systems that can perform tasks that typically require human intelligence. AI works by processing large amounts of data and identifying patterns to make decisions, predictions, or generate responses without being explicitly programmed for every scenario. Machine Learning is a subset of AI where systems improve their performance over time by learning from data. Deep Learning uses neural networks—structures inspired by how the human brain works—to process complex information. Large Language Models (LLMs) like GPT-4 are AI systems trained on billions of words from the internet, allowing them to understand and generate human-like text. Embeddings are a technique that converts text or images into numerical representations so computers can measure similarity between pieces of information. RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) is an advanced technique that combines document retrieval with LLMs—first finding relevant documents, then using an AI model to generate answers based on those specific documents. In your LangChain project, you're learning to build practical AI applications using these concepts to create chatbots, parse structured data from unstructured text, and build systems that can intelligently retrieve and process information.
